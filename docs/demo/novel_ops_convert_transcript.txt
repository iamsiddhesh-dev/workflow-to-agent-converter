# Expense Report Processing  [ops]
Process expense reports by verifying receipts and thresholds, routing high-value reports for approval, and generating monthly finance summaries.
Trigger: An expense report is submitted.
Pattern: router

Agents:
  - validator (Expense Validator): Verify receipt inclusion and determine if a report is valid, requires approval, or needs to be kicked back.
  - finance_processor (Finance Processor): Log approved expenses for reimbursement and generate the monthly department-level report.
  - manager (Department Manager): Approve or reject expense reports exceeding the $500 threshold.

Tasks:
  - validate_report @validator: Examine the expense report for a valid receipt. If missing, flag as 'kicked back'. If present, determine if it is under $500 (auto-reimburse) or over $500 (needs manager approval).
      -> Status of the report (auto-approved, pending_manager_approval, or kicked_back).
  - kickback_report @validator <- validate_report: Notify the submitter that their report is missing a receipt and has been kicked back for revision.
      -> A notification sent to the submitter.
  - approve_report @manager <- validate_report [human checkpoint]: Review and approve/deny expense reports exceeding $500.
      -> Approval or rejection decision for the expense report.
  - log_reimbursement @finance_processor <- validate_report, approve_report: Log the expense for final reimbursement once it is either auto-approved or manager-approved.
      -> Logged expense transaction.
  - generate_monthly_report @finance_processor: Create a monthly rollup of approved expenses, kicked-back reports, and total spend by department.
      -> Monthly finance summary document.

Tools:
  - notification service [builtin]: Send messages to submitters when reports are kicked back.
  - ledger system [external]: Log approved expenses and retrieve data for the monthly rollup.

Assumptions:
  - Manager identification for approval is handled via the organizational hierarchy metadata associated with the submitter.
  - The monthly report includes 'kicked back' status as a separate category of activity.
  - The finance processor runs the monthly rollup as an independent task from individual expense processing.
